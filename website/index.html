SAED Digital Twin Findings

Economic dispatch ensures electricity demand is served optimally across grid components. By simulating this using a digital twin calibrated with ten years of historical PGCB grid data (2015–2025), we analyzed operational patterns, cost boundaries, and limits governing Bangladesh's transmission network.

1. Operational Savings on Expensive Fuel Peak Units

The grid historically relied heavily on liquid fuel peaking generators costing $180/MWh. Under the SAED optimal merit-order dispatch, liquid fuel generation is mostly bypassed in 2025, substituting cheaper gas ($45/MWh) and coal ($65/MWh). This reveals significant recoverable grid efficiencies.

Liquid Fuel cost ($180/MWh) vs Gas ($45/MWh)

2. Governing Power with Seasonality Coefficients (α Matrix)

Availability metrics dictate the seasonal fuel mix. Hydroelectric dispatch ranges from 41% availability in winter (due to low water levels) up to 100% capacity in the monsoon season. Solar peaks in the pre-monsoon (100%) but scales back to 69% in the monsoon due to cloud cover.

Hydro limits: α = 0.41 (Winter) → 1.00 (Monsoon)

3. Grid Safety Breach during Monsoon Peaks by ~2030

Bangladesh's grid demand grows log-linearly at ~7.9% per year. Stress test projections demonstrate that reserve margins are breached below the safety planning floor of 8% first during high-temperature monsoon peak evenings around the years 2029–2030.

Safety Limit violation forecast: Year ~2029–2030

Calibration Metrics

When validating the SAED digital twin's optimal merit-order dispatch results against historical generation mixes, the mean absolute error (MAE) ranges between 581 MW and 1,036 MW depending on the season. This represents a deliberate divergence, reflecting real-world fuel supply constraints, transmission congestion, and scheduling delays.

Season MAE (MW) Interpretation
Winter 581 MW Stable base load operation, minimal peaking required.
Pre-monsoon 902 MW Transition period, solar ramp updates active.
Monsoon 1,036 MW Highest load uncertainty, peaking dispatch active.
Post-monsoon 775 MW Post-peak cool down, stable dispatch.
